Sketches

head-wind in the window drain

This drawing was done while on the move, during my summer 2006 travels through Europe after finishing a study abroad in London that spring. It was inspired after seeing the Salvador Dali exhibit in London. I worked on this on and off, in very small time increments; on the fairyboat crossing the English Channel, on the train ride through the Netherlands, Belgium, and even part of France. I can remember almost every time I worked on this particular piece because each time I did, I would mark the time, date and city I was in. So this one took a while before I was able to call it finished.

hand-picked steeple

This one was done while sitting on a park bench outside a London cafe. I can't remember its name, but it's on the boardwalk about half kilometer from the New Globe Theatre.

untitled (unfinished)

Spoilers! This one is still in the making. So far, I have used a variety of colored pencils, and charcoal pastels.

Pastels & Paints

blue flower

This is drawing two of three in a series. It depicts the head of a rose, and was done on rice paper, using oil pastels, charcoal pastels, and oil paints to add an extra layer of glossy texture.

green flower (unfinished)

This is drawing three (of three) in the "color" flower series. It is still unfinished. And although I've been told it resembles a head of lettuce, it's supposed to be the head of a flower - as the one above. But then again, it's art, so I guess it really could be a head of lettuce to some. Like the other two in this series, it was done on rice paper, with charcoal pastels, oil pastels, and oil paints.

magazine staircase

The old distorted staircase. This one was done a long time back, using only charcoal pastels, and a light coating of workable fixatif applied at the end.

torn

First sketched lightly on rice paper, then outlined in light charcoal. Watercolor was added later on. The bright spots are meant to look like reflections from the sun - but are actually not (despite the picture being a little blurry). The paper was torn in two pieces, and I stitched them together with a short length of yarn - hence the name "Torn."
